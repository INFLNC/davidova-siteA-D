— Project

A teddy on a blue kitchen morning. Visualization and lifestyle campaign for Vobro's milk chocolate and rice-grain bear range — from pack to playground.

Gryzzzli is Vobro's entry into children's confectionery — a milk chocolate bear with puffed rice grains, sold in both flowpack singles and 1 kg stabilopacks. The pack design was finalized by the client; the task was to build a complete visual system around it — shelf-ready CGI, lifestyle scenes and a campaign vocabulary that speaks to parents and children simultaneously. Built entirely in Blender with composited lifestyle environments.

— Concept

"The pack walks into its own world."

The visualization doesn't copy the label — it extends it. Strawberries, bananas, milk, granola — every supporting object on set is a deliberate echo of an illustration already on the artwork. The doypack becomes the protagonist of a breakfast scene it was always meant to inhabit.

Two formats. Two audiences. One bear.

The flowpack is impulse — a single bear that fits in a backpack, a jeans pocket, a lunchbox. It's the child's format: tactile, portable, personal. The visualization for flowpacks is diagonal, energetic, patterned — the bear repeated in motion.

The stabilopack is intent — a 1 kg family bag that lives on a kitchen shelf. It's the parent's format: reliable, generous, quiet. The visualization for stabilopacks is grounded, domestic, warm — the bag photographed in a real breakfast environment.

Both formats speak the same visual language: sky-blue field, milk-wave horizon, chocolate-brown character. But the tone shifts between motion and stillness, playground and kitchen, child and parent.

— Approach

"Visualization, not redesign — letting the finished pack lead the scene."

The brand identity and pack artwork were finalized by Vobro. The creative task was translation, not transformation — to build a world around a product that already knew its story, and let the CGI extend it into spaces the flat label can't reach.
