— About / Studio

A practice built around objects, light, and the time it takes to get them right.

— Studio

Alexandra Davidova works in CGI the way a director works in film — through light, composition and intent. Based in Warsaw and available internationally, she builds image systems for brands that treat their products as objects of design rather than units to photograph.

Her practice spans fragrance, beauty, packaging, fashion and tech — always with a preference for restraint over effect. Each project starts from a single question: what is this object, and what light does it deserve?

She collaborates with agencies, in-house creative teams and founders on campaigns, launches and long-form visual identities. Currently available for projects through 2026.

— Principles / How we work

Four rules.

  • 01 / 04

    Direction before render

    Every project starts with a brief, references and a written intent. The image follows the idea, not the other way around.

  • 02 / 04

    One source of light

    A lighting decision is a directing decision. We start with a single key, and only add what the frame can't live without.

  • 03 / 04

    Materials as language

    Surface tells the story. Glass is not a material — it's a sentence about heaviness, clarity and time.

  • 04 / 04

    Slow is fast

    Considered process produces images that don't need explaining. We work in days, not in hours, and it shows in the frame.
